# Attention vs Technology — Sourced Research Data

## HUMAN SIDE — Declining

### Task-Switching (Gloria Mark, UC Irvine)
- 2004: 2.5 minutes on a single screen | Gloria Mark & Victor Gonzalez, UC Irvine
- 2012: ~75 seconds (estimated midpoint) | Mark's longitudinal tracking
- 2016-2020: 47 seconds | "Attention Span" book (2023)
- Takes 25 min 26 sec to return to a task after interruption

### Screen Time
- 2013: ~6h 9m/day global (ages 16-64) | DataReportal/GWI
- 2019: 6h 30m/day global | DataReportal
- Q3 2024: 6h 38m/day global | DataReportal
- 2024 US adults: 7h 3m/day | Cross River Therapy/eMarketer
- 2019 US phone: 3h 43m | eMarketer
- 2024 US phone: 4h 30m | ConsumerAffairs

### Content Format Compression
**Pop Songs (Billboard Hot 100):**
- 1990s: 4:14 | Washington Post (2024)
- 2000: ~4:10 | Washington Post/Statista
- 2010-2015: 3:45 (225 sec) | Chartmetric/Statista
- 2016-2021: 3:18 (198 sec) | Chartmetric
- 2020s avg: 3:15 | Washington Post
- Songs shed ~40 seconds since 2000. Bridges being eliminated.

**Film Shot Length (James Cutting, Cornell):**
- 1930s: ~12 sec | Cutting et al.
- 1950s: ~8-10 sec | "Quicker, faster, darker" (2012)
- 1970s: ~6-7 sec | Cutting longitudinal
- 1990s: ~4-5 sec | Analysis of 160 films
- 2010s: ~3-4 sec | Psychological Science (2010)
- 2020s: ~2.5 sec | Cutting's latest

**TikTok:**
- Avg video length: 42.7 sec (2024) | Statista/Dash Social
- Avg watch before scroll: 8.4-17 sec | Shortimize/TTS Vibes
- Only ~10% watch to completion | Shortimize

### Reading Habits
- Books/year US: 15.6 (2016) → 12.6 (2022) | Gallup
- Read for pleasure: 28% of days (2000s) → 16% (2023) | American Time Use Survey
- Read a book in past year: 61% (1992) → 49% (2022) | Gallup

### Patience/Tolerance
**Page Load:**
- 53% of mobile users abandon at 3 sec | Google/SOASTA (2016)
- 1→3 sec: bounce +32%; 1→5 sec: +90%; 1→10 sec: +123% | Google (2017)

**Playback Speed:**
- 1.5x most popular non-normal speed on YouTube | YouTube Blog (2022)
- 41.3% use 2x on mobile video apps | Nature Scientific Reports (2025)

**Skip Intro:**
- 136 million presses/day | Netflix Engineering (2022)
- Saved cumulative 195 years | Netflix/News24 (2023)

### Messaging & Notification Overload
**Notifications:**
- Gen Z: 181/day (one every 8 min) | Common Sense Media (2023)
- Teens: 237/day (median 46) | Common Sense Media

**Phone Checks:**
- 2023: 144 times/day | Survey
- 2024: 205 times/day (+42% YoY) | ITBEAR/NBC
- Millennials 2024: 324 times/day | Same survey

**Message Volume:**
- WhatsApp: 100-150 billion/day | WhatsApp/Statista
- Email: 347.3 billion/day (2024) | Radicati Group
- Slack: 1.5 billion/day, 92 messages/user/day | Slack/DemandSage
- Tweets: 500 million/day | X/DemandSage

**Response Expectations:**
- Text (business): 42% expect within 15 min | EZ Texting 2024
- Text (personal): 64% expect <2 min | 2013 study
- Email: 89% expect within 1 hour | Toister Performance
- Live chat: under 30 seconds | Help Scout

### Cognitive Impacts
**Brain Drain (Ward et al., 2017):**
- Phone on desk = worse cognitive performance vs phone in another room
- Effect occurs even when phone face-down and OFF
- Published in Journal of the Association for Consumer Research

**Test Scores (NAEP):**
- Age 9 reading 2020→2022: -5 points (largest since 1990)
- Age 9 math 2020→2022: -7 points (first ever decline)
- Caveat: pandemic is major confound

**PISA (OECD 2022):**
- US math 2003→2022: -18 points
- Global reading 2018→2022: -10 points
- Global math 2018→2022: -15 points

### DEBUNKED: Goldfish Myth
- "8-second attention span shorter than goldfish" = FABRICATED
- Microsoft 2015 report pulled from unreliable Statistic Brain site
- BBC investigated: no source found
- Do NOT use. Use Gloria Mark's 47-second figure instead.

## TECHNOLOGY SIDE — Expanding

### Bot Traffic (Imperva Bad Bot Reports)
- 2013: Tracking begins, ~24% bad bots
- 2022: 47.4% total bots, 30.2% bad
- 2023: 49.6% total bots, 32% bad
- **2024: 51% bots > 49% human (FIRST TIME)**
- Bad bots alone: 37% of all traffic (2024)

### AI-Generated Content
- Nov 2024: 50.3% of new web articles AI-generated | Graphite.io
- Reddit AI posts: 15% (2025, up from 13% 2024) | Originality.ai
- Europol projection: 90% synthetic by 2026
- Deepfakes: doubled every 6 months, 550% increase 2019-2024

### AI Model Growth
**Parameters:**
- GPT-1 (2018): 117M
- GPT-2 (2019): 1.5B
- GPT-3 (2020): 175B
- GPT-4 (2023): ~1.76T (estimated, MoE)
- GPT-1→GPT-3 = 1,500x in 2 years

**API Pricing (cost collapse):**
- GPT-4 (2023): $60/M output tokens
- GPT-4o mini (2024): $0.60/M output tokens
- = 97%+ cost reduction in ~18 months

### Content Volume
- YouTube: 500+ hrs uploaded/minute
- YouTube total: 5.1 billion videos
- YouTube uploads/day: 20 million (2025, +38% YoY)
- Twitter/X: 500 million tweets/day
- Instagram: 95-100 million photos/videos daily
- WhatsApp: 100-150 billion messages/day
- Email: 347.3 billion/day

### Dark Patterns
- FTC/ICPEN 2024: 76% of subscription sites use dark patterns
- 67% use multiple dark patterns
- 81% use sneaking for auto-renewal
- Princeton crawl: 11.1% of 11K shopping sites

### AI Replacing Human Interactions
**Customer Service:**
- 85% of CS leaders piloting GenAI (2025) | Gartner
- Projected 80% autonomous by 2029 | Gartner
- 64% of customers prefer companies NOT use AI for CS | Gartner

**Content Moderation:**
- Meta: 97% hate speech caught by AI before reports
- Meta: 99%+ terrorism content by AI
- YouTube: 94% COVID misinformation by AI
- Twitter/X: 73% violations first flagged by AI

**Journalism:**
- AP: 10x more earnings reports via automation
- 50% of newsrooms using GenAI tools | WAN

### Attention Economy
**Digital Ad Market:**
- 2017: ~$230B → 2024: ~$790B
- Digital = 72.7% of all ad spend (2024)

**Value of Human Attention:**
- Social media: $0.25/hour | McKinsey
- Live sports: $33/hour | McKinsey
- Average across media: ~$2.50/hour | Kevin Kelly
